標題[問題] 為什麼 越落後的國家 紅綠燈就會倒數？
時間Fri Oct 28 22:54:34 2016
在台灣，很多行車管制號誌(三色燈，俗稱紅綠燈)都有紅燈倒數裝置， 而綠燈不倒數 匝道儀控則紅燈、綠燈都會倒數 全世界很多國家像東南亞各國(除新加坡)、中東、南美、俄羅斯、中國等 ，紅燈、綠燈 都會倒數 中國有些地方甚至連黃燈都會倒數， 而且倒數面板還做得很大一面 但是反觀歐美、日本、韓國、香港、新加坡、紐澳等先進已開發國家，他們的行車管制號 誌卻完全不會倒數 呈現了一個較落後、交通較亂的國家或地區，行車號誌就比較會倒數； 相反地，已開發、先進、交通較完善的國家或地區，行車號誌就比較不會倒數 到底是什麼原因導致 落後地方行車號誌會倒數 、 開發地方行車號誌不會倒數? 另外就是， 交通部運研所在8年前左右不是有研究說綠燈倒數會導致車禍肇事率上升嗎？ 為什麼中國、東南亞等國的號誌不管紅燈還是綠燈都會倒數呢？ 難道他們的行車秩序比我們「更好」？ 還是他們根本不吃我們這套？ 到底是有什麼原因呢？ 感恩 http://i.redwh.al/YY.jpg

This means that we neither allow the counting down of the green si gnal display nor the red signal display in a traffic signal face. Paragraphs 16 and 17 in Section 4D.26 explicitly prohibit the counting down of green signal indications and any other type of pre-yellow warning and provide a supporting statement describing why this prohibition is present. The freque ntly-asked question and answer available at the following link to our MUTCD we bsite also addresses this topic:http://mutcd.fhwa.dot.gov/knowledge/faqs/faq_p art4.htm#tcsfq6. The research mentioned in Paragraph 17 and in the answer to t he frequently-asked question is attached to this message. We do not have any research regarding the prohibition about counting down the red signal indication. We do not believe that there are any benefits to be rea lized by counting down the red signal indication, but we are aware of several disadvantages of doing so. Among the disadvantages are: *We have a lot of actuated signals in the United States and it is frequently difficult or impossible to know how many seconds of red are remaining at any g iven time. * We prefer that drivers continue to monitor the traffic conditions at the int ersection while waiting for a red signal indication to turn to green. We want them to be aware of such things as other traffic using the intersection, pede strians that might step into the crosswalk late, and any emergency vehicles th at might be approaching. Providing information as to how many seconds of red a re remaining could encourage drivers to temporarily focus on other things such as their smart phones. * We do not want the drivers in the first vehicles at the stop line to take th eir foot off of the brake and be ready to hit the gas pedal as soon as the lig ht changes to green. We believe that the driver’s slower response time when the light first changes to green contributes to intersection safety. When the light first changes to green, the driver is still legally obligated to yield t he right-of-way to any pedestrians or vehicles who are lawfully within the int ersection from the previous phase.
